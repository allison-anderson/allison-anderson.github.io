Allison Anderson

Coloratura Soprano | Vocal Instructor

A recent graduate from Carnegie Mellon University’s School of Music, Allison is excited to begin her first year as a Masters student at the College-Conservatory of Music, CCM this fall 2017. Her most recent roles at CMU Opera include the title role in Cavalli’s La Calisto, Adele/Die Fledermaus, and Zerlina/Don Giovanni (scenes).

A native of Houston, TX, Ms. Anderson grew up singing with Theater Under the Stars’ Humphrey’s School. After many musical theater roles, she was invited to be a member of Houston Grand Opera’s High School Voice Studio in 2012-2013. After moving to Pittsburgh, she held her position as a Trinity Choral Scholar from 2014-2017. In the summer of 2014, Ms. Anderson participated in the James Madison University ‘Summer Opera in Germany Program’, making her European debut as Chorus/Die Zigeunerbaron with the Middle-Saxony Opera House, and Spirit and Sailor/Dido and Aeneas. In summer 2016, she participated in Cincinnati’s College-Conservatory of Music Summer Opera Program, where she appeared as Suor Genovieffa/Suor Angelica, and Chorus/La Traviata. This past December, Allison was the soprano soloist for Handel’s Messiah with the Bach Choir of Pittsburgh under the baton of Thomas Douglas.

In 2013, Ms. Anderson was the recipient of the Best Leading Actress award at Houston’s Tommy Tune Awards for her performance as Nellie Forbush/South Pacific. Ms. Anderson was then qualified to compete at The National High School Musical Theater Awards (The Jimmy Awards) on the Minskoff stage in New York City.

Allison served as the 2016-2017 Music Director for the CMU Treblemakers, an up-and-coming a cappella group with members ranging from Computer Science to Biology majors. Under her direction, the Treblemakers earned the 1st place award at the 2017 ICCA Central Quarterfinals competition. In addition to leadership and management skills, this endeavor has led her to develop new ways of teaching. In this past academic year, she has maintained a voice studio in Pittsburgh consisting of six students, ages ranging from 9-22yrs old. Her newly found passion for teaching has inspired her to open a private summer voice studio in Houston, TX before beginning her Masters at CCM.

Her voice teachers include Daniel Teadt, with whom she previously studied with at Carnegie Mellon, and is a current student of Amy Johnson at CCM.
